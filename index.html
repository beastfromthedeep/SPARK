Mission Overview

The SPARK mission aims to study energetic particles in stellar atmospheres, focusing on how ions and electrons are accelerated and how energy is transported and dissipated in magnetized plasma.

  • How does magnetic energy accelerate particles?
  • How is energy transported and dissipated?
  • What roles do waves and plasma instabilities play?

Instruments

  • LISSAN: γ-ray and X-ray spectroscopic imager (4 keV - 100 MeV)
  • HiFI: High-resolution EUV imager with 100 ms time resolution
  • SISA: EUV integral field spectrograph with 2D spectral imaging

Mission Configuration

SPARK is a single spacecraft mission with a proposed launch in 2041 using Ariane 62. It will orbit around the L1 Lagrange point for continuous solar observation.

  • Payload mass: 573 kg
  • Orbit: L1 or LEO
  • Launch vehicle: Ariane 62
  • Mission duration: 6 years nominal + 3 years extended

Consortium

The SPARK consortium includes institutions from the UK, France, Switzerland, Belgium, Germany, Italy, Poland, Czech Republic, Spain, Ireland, and Austria.

Led by UCL-MSSL, the consortium has extensive experience in developing scientific instrument hardware for space missions.
